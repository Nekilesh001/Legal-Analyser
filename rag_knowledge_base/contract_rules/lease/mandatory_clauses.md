# Lease Agreement — Mandatory Clauses Under Indian Law

## Clause: Registration of Lease Deed for Terms Over 12 Months
Act: Registration Act 1908, Section 17; Transfer of Property Act 1882, Section 107
Contract type: lease
Requirement: A lease of immovable property for a term exceeding one year (or a lease reserving a yearly rent) must be registered under the Registration Act 1908. An unregistered lease exceeding 12 months is inadmissible as evidence of the lease terms and cannot be used to enforce rights under it.
Standard counter-clause: "This lease agreement shall be duly registered with the Sub-Registrar of Assurances having jurisdiction over the property, within 30 days of execution. The cost of stamp duty and registration shall be borne equally by the Landlord and Tenant unless otherwise agreed. An unregistered copy shall serve only for the 11-month term if registration is not completed."

## Clause: Stamp Duty Compliance
Act: Indian Stamp Act 1899; State Stamp Acts (e.g., Tamil Nadu Stamp Act)
Contract type: lease
Requirement: Lease agreements must be executed on stamp paper of adequate value as prescribed by the State Stamp Act. Stamp duty is calculated on the total rent (and deposit) for the lease period. An inadequately stamped lease is inadmissible in court proceedings.
Standard counter-clause: "This lease agreement shall be executed on non-judicial stamp paper of value not less than the amount prescribed by the applicable State Stamp Act, calculated on the basis of total annual rent. The parties shall ensure adequate stamping before execution."

## Clause: Detailed Description of the Leased Premises
Act: Transfer of Property Act 1882, Section 105; Registration Act 1908
Contract type: lease
Requirement: The leased property must be identified with sufficient particularity: property address, survey number, total area, specific floors/units if in a building, and any included amenities (parking, storage). Vague descriptions create disputes about the scope of the lease.
Standard counter-clause: "The Landlord agrees to lease to the Tenant the property situated at [complete address], bearing Survey/Door No. [number], admeasuring approximately [area] square feet / square meters, comprising [description of floors/units], together with [parking spaces/storage/amenities if any], as more particularly described in the attached Schedule A."

## Clause: Rent Amount, Payment Date, and Mode of Payment
Act: Transfer of Property Act 1882; Payment of Wages Act (for residential leases)
Contract type: lease
Requirement: The monthly rent, due date, mode of payment (bank transfer/cheque), bank account details, and consequences of late payment must be specified. TDS deduction requirements must be addressed for rents exceeding Rs. 50,000/month.
Standard counter-clause: "The monthly rent of INR [amount] shall be paid by the Tenant to the Landlord by [NEFT/RTGS/cheque] on or before the [5th/10th] day of each month for that month. The Landlord's bank details are: [account details]. Late payment beyond 10 days shall attract interest at [rate]% per month."

## Clause: Security Deposit Amount and Refund Conditions
Act: State-specific Rent Control Acts; Indian Contract Act 1872
Contract type: lease
Requirement: The security deposit amount (typically 2–10 months' rent), the conditions under which it may be deducted, and the timeline for refund must be explicitly specified. In many states (Tamil Nadu, Karnataka), the Rent Control Act limits security deposits and prescribes refund timelines.
Standard counter-clause: "The Tenant shall pay a refundable security deposit of INR [amount] (equivalent to [X] months' rent) to the Landlord upon execution. The security deposit shall be refunded within 30 days of vacation of the premises, after deducting any dues, unpaid rent, or cost of repairing damages attributable to the Tenant (ordinary wear and tear excluded)."

## Clause: Permitted Use of Property
Act: Transfer of Property Act 1882; local municipal regulations
Contract type: lease
Requirement: The lease must specify whether the property is for residential, commercial, or specific business use. Use for an unauthorized purpose (e.g., using a residential property for commercial purposes) violates municipal laws and may give grounds for eviction.
Standard counter-clause: "The Tenant shall use the leased premises exclusively for [residential purposes / office purposes of the Tenant's business / retail of specified goods]. The Tenant shall not use the premises for any illegal activity, for storing hazardous materials, or for any purpose not permitted under applicable municipal and zoning regulations."

## Clause: Maintenance and Repair Responsibilities
Act: Transfer of Property Act 1882, Section 108(m)(n)
Contract type: lease
Requirement: Under Section 108, the landlord is responsible for maintaining the premises in a tenantable condition (structural repairs), while the tenant is responsible for keeping the interior in good condition. The lease should clearly allocate specific repair responsibilities.
Standard counter-clause: "The Landlord shall be responsible for all major structural repairs, maintenance of the building's common areas, and repair of electrical, plumbing, and water supply systems (excluding damage caused by Tenant misuse). The Tenant shall be responsible for minor interior maintenance, keeping the premises in clean condition, and repairing any damage attributable to the Tenant's use."

## Clause: Rent Increase Mechanism and Notice
Act: State Rent Control Acts; Indian Contract Act 1872
Contract type: lease
Requirement: The basis and timing of rent increases must be agreed upon. In states with Rent Control Acts (Tamil Nadu, Maharashtra, Delhi), rent increases are regulated. For commercial leases, the escalation rate and notice period should be specified.
Standard counter-clause: "The monthly rent shall be subject to an annual escalation of [5-10]% on each anniversary of the commencement date, with thirty days' prior written notice to the Tenant. For commercial leases, escalation shall be benchmarked to the Consumer Price Index (CPI) or a fixed rate, whichever is lower."

## Clause: Subletting and Assignment Restrictions
Act: Transfer of Property Act 1882, Section 108(j)
Contract type: lease
Requirement: Under Section 108(j), a tenant may sublet if not prohibited. To prevent subletting, the lease must expressly prohibit it. The conditions for permitting subletting with landlord consent should also be specified.
Standard counter-clause: "The Tenant shall not sublet, assign, or transfer the leased premises or any part thereof to any third party without the prior written consent of the Landlord. The Landlord shall not unreasonably withhold consent for subletting to a creditworthy subtenant. Any permitted subletting shall not relieve the Tenant of their obligations under this lease."

## Clause: Notice Period for Vacation and Termination
Act: Transfer of Property Act 1882, Section 106
Contract type: lease
Requirement: Section 106 prescribes default notice periods: 15 days for month-to-month tenancy in towns (30 days for agricultural leases). The lease should specify a notice period that meets or exceeds the statutory minimum. Notice should be in writing.
Standard counter-clause: "Either party may terminate this lease at the end of the agreed term, or thereafter, by providing written notice of at least [60] days. Notice shall be sent by registered post or courier to the addresses specified herein. The Tenant shall vacate and return the premises in good condition (except for ordinary wear and tear) within [7] days of the notice period expiry."

## Clause: Landlord's Right to Enter and Inspect
Act: Transfer of Property Act 1882, Section 108(f)
Contract type: lease
Requirement: The landlord has a right to enter the property for reasonable inspection but must give adequate notice. Entering without notice (except in genuine emergencies) is a trespass. The lease should specify the notice requirement for inspection.
Standard counter-clause: "The Landlord or their authorized representative may inspect the leased premises with at least 48 hours prior written notice to the Tenant during reasonable business hours. In case of emergency (fire, flood, structural danger), the Landlord may enter without notice. Such inspection shall not unreasonably interfere with the Tenant's use."

## Clause: Utilities, Property Tax, and Common Area Charges
Act: Electricity Act 2003; local municipal law; Transfer of Property Act 1882
Contract type: lease
Requirement: All utilities (electricity, water, gas) and applicable charges (property tax, maintenance charges, society fees) must be allocated between landlord and tenant. Leaving this ambiguous leads to disputes, especially for commercial properties.
Standard counter-clause: "The Tenant shall pay all utility charges (electricity, water, gas, internet) consumed within the leased premises directly to the service provider. The Landlord shall pay all property taxes assessed on the premises. Society maintenance charges (if applicable) shall be paid by [Landlord/Tenant] and a copy of the payment receipt provided monthly."

## Clause: Handover Condition and Inventory
Act: Indian Contract Act 1872; Transfer of Property Act 1882
Contract type: lease
Requirement: The initial condition of the property and all fixtures/fittings must be documented (preferably with photographs) at the time of handover to prevent disputes at vacation. An inventory list is essential for furnished properties.
Standard counter-clause: "The Landlord shall hand over the leased premises to the Tenant in a clean and tenantable condition on the commencement date. Both parties shall jointly inspect the premises and execute an inventory and condition report (including photographs) to be attached as Schedule B. Any pre-existing defects shall be noted in Schedule B and shall not be charged to the Tenant at vacation."

## Clause: Renewal Terms and Right of First Refusal
Act: Transfer of Property Act 1882; Indian Contract Act 1872
Contract type: lease
Requirement: An option to renew should specify: the notice period to exercise the option, the basis for rent revision on renewal, and whether the same terms apply. Without a defined renewal process, the tenant must renegotiate from scratch.
Standard counter-clause: "The Tenant shall have a right of first refusal to renew this lease for a further term of [11 months / 2 years] on the same terms (with the applicable rent escalation) by giving written notice to the Landlord at least 60 days before the expiry of the current term. If the Tenant does not exercise this option, the Landlord may offer the premises to third parties."

## Clause: Governing Law, Dispute Resolution, and Jurisdiction
Act: Transfer of Property Act 1882; Arbitration and Conciliation Act 1996; State Rent Control Acts
Contract type: lease
Requirement: For commercial leases, arbitration is appropriate. For residential leases, the applicable Rent Control Court (in states with rent control legislation) may have exclusive jurisdiction for certain disputes (eviction, excess rent). The lease should clarify which forum applies to which type of dispute.
Standard counter-clause: "This lease shall be governed by the laws of India and the laws of the State of [state]. Commercial disputes shall be referred to arbitration under the Arbitration and Conciliation Act 1996. Disputes concerning eviction, rent fixation, or tenant protections that fall within the jurisdiction of the Rent Control Authority shall be referred to the appropriate Rent Control Court."
