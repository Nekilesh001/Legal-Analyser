# Lease Agreement — Negotiation Playbook

## Clause: Security Deposit More Than 3 Months Rent (Excessive)
Act: State Rent Control Acts; Mumbai Rent Act; Tamil Nadu Buildings (Lease and Rent Control) Act 1960
Contract type: lease
Requirement: Several state rent control acts cap security deposits. In Maharashtra (Mumbai), the security deposit is capped at 3 months for residential properties. In Tamil Nadu, the cap is 2 months. Demanding deposits of 10–12 months is exploitative and potentially illegal in these states.
Standard counter-clause: "The security deposit shall not exceed three months' rent for residential premises and six months' rent for commercial premises. The deposit shall be held interest-free by the Landlord and returned in full within 30 days of vacation, subject only to deductions for unpaid dues and documented damage attributable to the Tenant."

## Clause: Landlord May Terminate Without Notice (Arbitrary Eviction)
Act: Transfer of Property Act 1882, Section 106; State Rent Control Acts
Contract type: lease
Requirement: Under the Transfer of Property Act and state rent control acts, a landlord cannot evict a tenant without proper legal process in many circumstances. An arbitrary termination clause (without notice or for any reason) is contrary to the statutory protections afforded to tenants.
Standard counter-clause: "The Landlord may terminate this lease only for: (a) non-payment of rent for more than 30 days after due date; (b) material breach of lease terms not cured within 30 days of written notice; (c) end of the agreed lease term with 60 days notice; or (d) genuine requirement of the property by the Landlord for own use (with 90 days notice and supporting documentation)."

## Clause: Tenant Pays All Repair Costs Including Structural
Act: Transfer of Property Act 1882, Section 108(m)
Contract type: lease
Requirement: Section 108 of the Transfer of Property Act places structural repair responsibility on the landlord. A lease clause shifting all repair responsibility to the tenant (including structural, plumbing, electrical system repairs) is contrary to this statutory provision and may be voidable.
Standard counter-clause: "Structural repairs, repairs to the building's electrical wiring, plumbing mains, and water supply infrastructure shall be the Landlord's responsibility and completed within 30 days of notification. The Tenant is responsible for: minor interior repairs, fixtures installed by the Tenant, and repair of damage caused by the Tenant's misuse."

## Clause: Rent Escalation of More Than 10% Per Year
Act: State Rent Control Acts; Consumer Price Index benchmarks
Contract type: lease
Requirement: Under state rent control acts applicable in many cities, arbitrary rent increases above permitted levels are illegal. Even for commercial properties outside rent control, escalation beyond 10% annually (in an environment of 5-6% inflation) should be resisted and tied to an objective index.
Standard counter-clause: "Annual rent escalation shall not exceed 7% per annum for residential premises and 10% per annum for commercial premises, or the change in the All India Consumer Price Index (AICPI-Urban), whichever is lower. Any escalation shall take effect only after 30 days prior written notice."

## Clause: Forfeiture of Entire Deposit for Any Breach
Act: Indian Contract Act 1872, Section 74; Specific Relief Act 1963
Contract type: lease
Requirement: Forfeiture of the entire security deposit for minor or curable breaches is disproportionate and courts have held such penalty clauses unenforceable where the loss does not match the penalty. The deposit can only be applied against actual, documented losses.
Standard counter-clause: "The security deposit may be applied by the Landlord only against: (a) unpaid rent and utility dues; (b) cost of repairing actual damage attributable to the Tenant (with written quotes from three contractors); (c) cost of professional cleaning if the premises is left in an unsatisfactory condition. The balance, if any, shall be refunded within 30 days with an itemized account."

## Clause: No Right to Alter or Customize Premises
Act: Transfer of Property Act 1882, Section 108(h); Building Permission laws
Contract type: lease
Requirement: A blanket prohibition on all alterations leaves the tenant unable to customize the premises for business use. For commercial leases, partitions, cabling, and other non-structural alterations are standard requirements. The clause should distinguish between structural and non-structural alterations.
Standard counter-clause: "The Tenant may make non-structural alterations and fit-outs (including partitions, cabling, flooring, painting, and installation of fixtures) without Landlord's consent, provided such alterations do not compromise structural integrity. Structural alterations require prior written Landlord consent. All Tenant-installed fixtures shall be removed at vacation unless the Landlord elects to retain them."

## Clause: Landlord Can Access Property Without Notice
Act: Transfer of Property Act 1882; Indian Penal Code (trespass provisions)
Contract type: lease
Requirement: Entering leased premises without notice is trespass (criminal, not just civil) once the property is in the tenant's exclusive possession. The lease should require minimum 48-hour notice for inspection and only allow emergency access without notice.
Standard counter-clause: "The Landlord shall provide the Tenant with at least 48 hours' prior written notice (by SMS, email, or phone call confirmed in writing) before entering the leased premises for any inspection, repair, or showing to prospective tenants. Emergency access (fire, flood, structural collapse risk) may occur without notice, and the Landlord shall notify the Tenant as soon as practicable thereafter."

## Clause: Subletting Absolute Prohibition With No Exception
Act: Transfer of Property Act 1882, Section 108(j); commercial business continuity
Contract type: lease
Requirement: An absolute subletting prohibition without any exception for group companies, affiliates, or emergency situations may be commercially unrealistic for a business tenant. A right to sublet to affiliates and group companies without landlord consent (but with notification) is commercially standard.
Standard counter-clause: "The Tenant may sublet or license the premises to a group company or wholly-owned subsidiary of the Tenant without Landlord's prior consent, provided the Tenant gives 15 days prior written notice and the subtenant's use complies with the Permitted Use clause. For all other subletting, prior written consent of the Landlord is required, not to be unreasonably withheld."

## Clause: Tenant Responsible for Landlord's Property Tax
Act: Municipal Corporation Acts; Income Tax Act 1961 (TDS on rent)
Contract type: lease
Requirement: Property tax is a statutory liability of the property owner (landlord) under all municipal corporation acts. A lease clause requiring the tenant to pay the landlord's property tax constitutes an additional rent and the tenant is being made to bear a landlord's statutory obligation.
Standard counter-clause: "All property taxes, municipal corporation charges, and assessments levied on the leased property shall be the sole responsibility of the Landlord. Any increase in property tax during the lease term shall be borne by the Landlord. The Tenant is responsible only for taxes specifically applicable to the Tenant's business or occupation."

## Clause: No Compensation for Landlord's Delay in Handing Over Possession
Act: Indian Contract Act 1872, Section 73; Transfer of Property Act 1882
Contract type: lease
Requirement: If the landlord delays delivering vacant possession of the leased premises beyond the agreed commencement date, the tenant suffers real losses (alternative accommodation, moving costs, business disruption). Compensation for landlord's delay in possession should be specified.
Standard counter-clause: "If the Landlord fails to deliver vacant and habitable possession of the leased premises by the agreed commencement date, the commencement date shall be extended by the number of delayed days, and the Landlord shall compensate the Tenant at a rate of INR [amount] per day of delay, or abate the rent for the delay period, at the Tenant's election."

## Clause: Lock-In Period Binds Only the Tenant
Act: Indian Contract Act 1872, Section 14 (bilateral nature of contracts)
Contract type: lease
Requirement: A one-sided lock-in period where only the Tenant cannot exit while the Landlord retains the right to terminate is not a true lock-in and is inequitable. A lock-in should bind both parties equally.
Standard counter-clause: "During the lock-in period of [X] months from the commencement date, neither party may terminate this lease without the other's written consent. If the Tenant vacates during the lock-in period, the Tenant shall pay rent for the balance of the lock-in period. If the Landlord terminates during the lock-in period, the Landlord shall refund the security deposit and pay a termination fee equal to the balance lock-in period rent."

## Clause: Excessive Penalty for Late Payment (More Than 18% PA)
Act: Indian Contract Act 1872, Section 74; RBI reference rates
Contract type: lease
Requirement: Late payment interest exceeding the RBI prescribed rate (currently around 10%) or banking interest rates (18% PA is the typical legal upper limit for contractual interest in India) may be reduced by courts as penal rather than compensatory.
Standard counter-clause: "Late payment of rent beyond the due date shall attract simple interest at [12-15]% per annum on the overdue amount, calculated from the due date to the actual payment date. This interest shall be the Landlord's sole remedy for delayed payment and shall not be a ground for termination unless rent remains unpaid for more than 30 days."

## Clause: Renovation Costs Entirely on Tenant, Landlord Benefits on Vacation
Act: Indian Contract Act 1872; Transfer of Property Act 1882, Section 108
Contract type: lease
Requirement: Requiring tenants to renovate at their cost and then requiring removal or transferring improvements to the landlord at vacation without compensation creates a one-sided benefit. Either the landlord should contribute to approved improvements or commit to compensation on vacation.
Standard counter-clause: "Permanent improvements made by the Tenant with Landlord's prior written consent shall, at the Landlord's option: (a) be removed by the Tenant at no cost to the Landlord at vacation; or (b) remain as part of the premises, with the Landlord paying the Tenant the depreciated value of such improvements. Temporary fixtures installed by the Tenant may be removed by the Tenant at any time."

## Clause: Lease Granted for Fixed Term With No Right to Remain for Wind-Down
Act: Transfer of Property Act 1882; commercial leasing practice
Contract type: lease
Requirement: For commercial tenants, a sudden end of lease with no wind-down period can be devastating to business operations. A right to remain in the premises for a limited period beyond the lease term to complete an orderly move (at agreed rent) is commercially reasonable.
Standard counter-clause: "If the Tenant does not renew this lease, the Tenant shall be entitled to a wind-down period of up to [30/60] days beyond the expiry date on the same terms, by giving written notice of the election to the Landlord at least 30 days before the lease expiry. The Tenant shall pay monthly rent (at the prevailing rate) during the wind-down period."

## Clause: No Provision for Lease Modification Due to Government Acquisition
Act: Land Acquisition Act 2013; National Highways Act 1956; Municipal Corporation Acts
Contract type: lease
Requirement: If the leased property or a portion of it is acquired or requisitioned by a government authority during the lease term, the rights and obligations of both parties must be addressed. The lease should specify how rent is adjusted if part of the premises becomes unusable due to government action.
Standard counter-clause: "If all or a substantial part of the leased premises is permanently acquired or requisitioned by a government authority during the lease term: (a) this lease shall terminate upon the date of such acquisition; (b) the security deposit shall be refunded in full within 7 days; and (c) the Landlord shall claim compensation from the acquiring authority for any losses the Tenant demonstrates arose from the acquisition. Partial acquisition shall entitle the Tenant to a proportionate rent reduction."
