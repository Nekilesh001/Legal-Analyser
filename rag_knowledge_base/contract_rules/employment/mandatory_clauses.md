# Employment Contract — Mandatory Clauses Under Indian Law

## Clause: Appointment Letter and Terms of Employment
Act: Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act 1946, Section 3
Contract type: employment
Requirement: Every employer covered under the Act must issue written standing orders defining conditions of employment including designation, duties, hours, wages, and leave. Employees must receive a written appointment letter before or at the time of joining.
Standard counter-clause: "The Employer shall provide the Employee with a written appointment letter setting out designation, compensation, working hours, leave entitlement, and reporting structure, prior to or on the date of joining."

## Clause: Notice Period for Termination (Non-Probationary Employees)
Act: Industrial Disputes Act 1947, Section 25F
Contract type: employment
Requirement: Employees who have completed 1 or more years of continuous service must receive either one month's written notice (or wages in lieu) plus retrenchment compensation of 15 days' average pay per completed year of service.
Standard counter-clause: "Either party may terminate this agreement by providing thirty (30) days' written notice, or payment of an equivalent amount in lieu of notice. In case of retrenchment after one year of service, retrenchment compensation shall be paid as per Section 25F of the Industrial Disputes Act 1947."

## Clause: Minimum Wages Compliance
Act: Minimum Wages Act 1948
Contract type: employment
Requirement: No employer shall pay wages to an employee at a rate less than the minimum wages fixed by the appropriate government for the relevant scheduled employment and category. The minimum wage must be reviewed periodically.
Standard counter-clause: "The Employee's total compensation shall at all times meet or exceed the minimum wages as notified by the State Government for the applicable category of employment under the Minimum Wages Act 1948."

## Clause: Payment of Wages Timely Manner
Act: Payment of Wages Act 1936, Section 5
Contract type: employment
Requirement: Wages must be paid on a working day within 7 days of the wage period end (10 days if more than 1000 employees). For monthly wage periods, payment must be before the 7th or 10th of the following month.
Standard counter-clause: "The Employee's monthly compensation shall be credited to their designated bank account on or before the 7th day of the following month for the preceding wage period."

## Clause: Provident Fund Contribution
Act: Employees' Provident Funds and Miscellaneous Provisions Act 1952, Section 6
Contract type: employment
Requirement: Both employer and employee must contribute 12% of basic wages (including dearness allowance) to the EPF. Applicable to establishments with 20 or more employees. The employer's contribution is in addition to the employee's gross salary.
Standard counter-clause: "Provident fund contributions shall be deducted from the Employee's salary and remitted to the EPFO in accordance with the Employees' Provident Funds and Miscellaneous Provisions Act 1952. The employer shall contribute an equal amount as required by law."

## Clause: Gratuity Entitlement
Act: Payment of Gratuity Act 1972, Section 4
Contract type: employment
Requirement: An employee who has completed 5 years of continuous service is entitled to gratuity at the rate of 15 days' wages for each completed year of service (or part thereof exceeding 6 months). Maximum gratuity payable is Rs. 20 lakhs.
Standard counter-clause: "The Employee shall be entitled to gratuity upon separation after completing five years of continuous service, calculated at 15 days' last drawn wages for each completed year of service, as per the Payment of Gratuity Act 1972."

## Clause: Maternity Benefit Provision
Act: Maternity Benefit Act 1961 (amended 2017), Section 5
Contract type: employment
Requirement: Female employees are entitled to 26 weeks of paid maternity leave (for the first two children; 12 weeks for subsequent children). Applicable to establishments employing 10 or more persons. Dismissal during maternity leave is prohibited.
Standard counter-clause: "Female employees shall be entitled to maternity benefits in accordance with the Maternity Benefit Act 1961, including 26 weeks of paid maternity leave for the first two children, and shall not be dismissed or discharged during such leave."

## Clause: Prevention of Sexual Harassment (POSH) Policy Reference
Act: Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace (Prevention, Prohibition and Redressal) Act 2013
Contract type: employment
Requirement: Every employer with 10 or more employees must constitute an Internal Complaints Committee (ICC). The employment contract must acknowledge the employer's POSH policy. Failure to constitute ICC attracts fine up to Rs. 50,000.
Standard counter-clause: "The Employer has constituted an Internal Complaints Committee as required under the Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace (Prevention, Prohibition and Redressal) Act 2013. The Employee acknowledges having received and read the Company's POSH Policy."

## Clause: Working Hours and Overtime
Act: Factories Act 1948, Section 51–56 / Shops and Establishments Acts (State-specific)
Contract type: employment
Requirement: Maximum working hours are 9 hours per day and 48 hours per week in factories. Overtime must be paid at double the ordinary rate. State Shops and Establishments Acts may apply to IT/service sector employees.
Standard counter-clause: "Normal working hours shall not exceed 9 hours per day or 48 hours per week. Any work beyond these limits shall be considered overtime and compensated at twice the ordinary hourly rate, in accordance with applicable law."

## Clause: Annual Leave and Earned Leave Entitlement
Act: Factories Act 1948, Section 79 / State Shops and Establishments Acts
Contract type: employment
Requirement: Workers who have worked 240 days are entitled to 1 day of earned leave for every 20 days worked (1 day per 15 days in some state acts). Leaves must be allowed to be accumulated and encashed on separation.
Standard counter-clause: "The Employee shall be entitled to earned leave at the rate of one day for every twenty working days, subject to a minimum of 12 days per year. Unused leave may be accumulated up to a maximum of 60 days and shall be encashed on resignation or retirement."

## Clause: ESI (Employee State Insurance) Applicability
Act: Employees' State Insurance Act 1948, Section 40
Contract type: employment
Requirement: Both employer (3.25% of wages) and employee (0.75% of wages) must contribute to ESI for employees earning up to Rs. 21,000/month. Applicable to establishments with 10 or more employees in notified areas.
Standard counter-clause: "Where applicable, ESI contributions shall be deducted from the Employee's wages and remitted along with the Employer's contribution to ESIC as per the Employees' State Insurance Act 1948."

## Clause: Non-Compete — Indian Law Limitations
Act: Indian Contract Act 1872, Section 27
Contract type: employment
Requirement: Under Section 27, agreements in restraint of trade are void in India. Non-compete clauses are ONLY enforceable during the employment term, not post-employment. Any post-employment restriction is generally unenforceable except in limited circumstances (e.g., trade secrets protection by injunction).
Standard counter-clause: "During the term of employment, the Employee shall not directly or indirectly engage in any activity that competes with the Employer's business. [NOTE: Post-employment restrictions are generally unenforceable under Indian law and are not included in this agreement.]"

## Clause: Bonus Payment Entitlement
Act: Payment of Bonus Act 1965, Section 10
Contract type: employment
Requirement: Every employee earning up to Rs. 21,000/month who has worked for 30 working days in an accounting year is entitled to a minimum bonus of 8.33% of wages (up to Rs. 7,000/month or minimum wage, whichever is higher). Maximum bonus is 20%.
Standard counter-clause: "The Employee shall be entitled to bonus as per the Payment of Bonus Act 1965. The minimum statutory bonus shall be 8.33% of the eligible wages or as computed under the Act, whichever is higher, payable within 8 months of the close of the accounting year."

## Clause: Professional Tax Deduction
Act: State Professional Tax Acts (e.g., Tamil Nadu Tax on Professions, Trades, Callings and Employments Act 1992)
Contract type: employment
Requirement: Employers must deduct professional tax from employees' salaries and remit to the state government. Rates vary by state and income slab. Failure to deduct/remit attracts penalties.
Standard counter-clause: "Professional tax as applicable under the law of the state in which the Employee is employed shall be deducted from the monthly salary and remitted to the appropriate state authority."

## Clause: Workplace Safety and Health
Act: Factories Act 1948 / Occupational Safety, Health and Working Conditions Code 2020
Contract type: employment
Requirement: Employers must maintain safe working conditions, provide safety equipment, conduct health check-ups, and ensure compliance with occupational health norms. For hazardous processes, additional medical examinations are mandatory.
Standard counter-clause: "The Employer shall maintain a safe and healthy workplace in accordance with the Factories Act 1948 and all other applicable occupational safety laws, and shall provide necessary safety equipment and training at no cost to the Employee."
