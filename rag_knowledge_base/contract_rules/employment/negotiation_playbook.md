# Employment Contract — Negotiation Playbook

## Clause: Unilateral Termination Without Notice
Act: Industrial Disputes Act 1947, Section 25F; Indian Contract Act 1872
Contract type: employment
Requirement: Termination without notice or wages in lieu is void for employees with 1+ year of service except in proven misconduct cases. A clause allowing the employer to terminate "at will" or "at sole discretion" without notice is likely unenforceable and constitutes unfair labour practice.
Standard counter-clause: "The Employer may terminate this agreement without notice only in cases of proven misconduct, fraud, or gross negligence as defined in the Company's disciplinary policy, which shall be followed before any such termination. In all other cases, thirty days' written notice or equivalent wages in lieu shall be provided to the Employee."

## Clause: Overly Broad IP Assignment
Act: Indian Contract Act 1872; Copyright Act 1957, Section 17
Contract type: employment
Requirement: Blanket assignment of all inventions (including those made outside working hours with personal resources) is unenforceable. IP assignment should be limited to work created in the course of employment using employer resources.
Standard counter-clause: "The Employee assigns to the Employer all intellectual property rights in works, inventions, and developments created during working hours, using Company resources, or directly related to the Company's business. Inventions created entirely on the Employee's own time, without Company resources, and unrelated to Company's business are excluded from this assignment."

## Clause: Excessive Non-Compete Duration Post-Employment
Act: Indian Contract Act 1872, Section 27
Contract type: employment
Requirement: Post-employment non-compete clauses are void under Section 27 in India. Courts have consistently refused enforcement unless tied to confidentiality and trade secret protection. Any such clause should be redrafted as a trade secret/confidentiality obligation instead.
Standard counter-clause: "The Employee agrees to maintain confidentiality of the Company's trade secrets, client information, and proprietary processes during employment and for a period of two years thereafter. [The post-employment non-compete restriction has been removed as it is void under Section 27 of the Indian Contract Act 1872.]"

## Clause: Variable Pay Entirely at Employer Discretion
Act: Payment of Wages Act 1936; Contract Act principles
Contract type: employment
Requirement: Variable pay components should have defined measurement criteria. A clause stating bonus or variable pay is "entirely at employer's sole discretion" creates ambiguity about payment obligation and may be unfair if targets are not defined.
Standard counter-clause: "Variable pay shall be calculated based on achievement of mutually agreed KPIs as set out in the performance plan for each review period. The minimum variable pay payable on 100% target achievement shall be [amount/percentage]. KPIs shall be communicated to the Employee within 30 days of the start of each performance period."

## Clause: Unreasonable Probation Extension
Act: Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act 1946
Contract type: employment
Requirement: Probation periods should be of reasonable duration and clearly defined. Indefinite or auto-renewing probation periods without employee consent or objective criteria are unfair. Total probation should not ordinarily exceed 6 months.
Standard counter-clause: "The probation period shall be three to six months from the date of joining. Any extension of probation must be communicated in writing with specific performance concerns at least 15 days before the scheduled confirmation date. Total probation including any extension shall not exceed 12 months."

## Clause: Full Clawback of Training Costs Without Cap
Act: Indian Contract Act 1872; Labour laws generally
Contract type: employment
Requirement: Training cost recovery bonds may be enforceable but must be reasonable. Courts typically disallow full cost recovery for general skill training. The bond period should be proportionate to training cost. Recovery of wages paid during training is generally void.
Standard counter-clause: "If the Employee leaves within [period] months of completing the [specific named training program], the Employee shall reimburse the Company for actual third-party training costs (excluding wages) pro-rated by the remaining months of the agreed service commitment, up to a maximum of INR [amount]."

## Clause: Mandatory Arbitration Without Employee Choice
Act: Arbitration and Conciliation Act 1996; Labour laws
Contract type: employment
Requirement: For employees covered under industrial law, mandatory arbitration that bypasses the industrial tribunal system may be legally challenged. Individual employment disputes may need to go through designated labour courts. Mutually agreed arbitration is generally valid for non-industrial employees.
Standard counter-clause: "Any dispute arising from this agreement not resolved within 30 days of written notice shall be referred to arbitration by a mutually appointed sole arbitrator in [city]. The costs of arbitration shall be borne equally. This clause does not bar the Employee from approaching statutory bodies such as the Labour Commissioner where applicable."

## Clause: Moonlighting Prohibition Without Carve-Out
Act: Indian Contract Act 1872; respective state IT/ITES policy
Contract type: employment
Requirement: An absolute ban on any outside activity (including personal freelancing, academic work, and non-competing activities) is often overly broad. Many courts in India recognize the right to use personal time for non-competing activities.
Standard counter-clause: "The Employee shall not undertake any employment, contract work, or advisory role with a direct or indirect competitor during the term of this agreement, without prior written approval. Non-competing personal activities (such as writing, teaching, or investing) undertaken in personal time without using Company resources are permitted."

## Clause: Annual Leave Cannot Be Carried Forward or Encashed
Act: Factories Act 1948, Section 79; State Shops Acts
Contract type: employment
Requirement: Under the Factories Act and most state shops acts, earned leave must be allowed to accumulate and must be encashed on separation. A blanket "use it or lose it" leave policy is unlawful under the Factories Act for factory workers, and unlawful under most state shops acts for office employees.
Standard counter-clause: "Unused earned leave shall be carried forward to the following year, subject to a maximum accumulation of 60 days. Leave in excess of 60 days shall be encashed at the end of each year at basic daily wage rate. On separation, all accumulated earned leave shall be encashed."

## Clause: Fixed-Term Contract Without Statutory Benefits
Act: Contract Labour (Regulation and Abolition) Act 1970; Fixed-Term Employment Notification 2018
Contract type: employment
Requirement: Under the Fixed-Term Employment Notification 2018 (applicable to all sectors), fixed-term employees are entitled to all statutory benefits on a pro-rata basis, including gratuity, even if employed for less than one year. Fixed-term contracts cannot be used to deny statutory benefits.
Standard counter-clause: "This fixed-term contract does not deprive the Employee of any statutory entitlements including gratuity (on pro-rata basis for each year of service as per Fixed-Term Employment Notification 2018), leave, bonus, EPF, ESI, or any other benefit mandated by applicable law."

## Clause: Employer-Only Right to Modify Terms
Act: Indian Contract Act 1872, Section 10 (free consent requirement)
Contract type: employment
Requirement: Unilateral right of the employer to modify salary, role, or location without employee consent is challengeable. Material changes to terms without consent may amount to constructive dismissal.
Standard counter-clause: "Any material change in the Employee's designation, compensation, location, or reporting structure shall require thirty days' written notice and the Employee's written consent. Minor operational changes within the same role and location do not require such consent."

## Clause: Over-Broad Jurisdiction and Governing Law
Act: Civil Procedure Code; Supreme Court guidance on jurisdiction clauses
Contract type: employment
Requirement: Jurisdiction clauses specifying only the employer's city of registration may be challenged if the employee works in a different city. Indian courts have held that jurisdiction lies where the cause of action arises, not only where the contract specifies.
Standard counter-clause: "Disputes shall be subject to the exclusive jurisdiction of courts in [city where the Employee is primarily based / or if different, both parties' cities]. This clause shall not prejudice the Employee's right to approach a Labour Court or Employment Tribunal of competent jurisdiction."

## Clause: Company Can Deduct Any Amount from Final Settlement
Act: Payment of Wages Act 1936, Section 7; Industrial Disputes Act 1947
Contract type: employment
Requirement: Authorized deductions from wages are limited by the Payment of Wages Act. Deducting more than 75% of wages in any wage period is prohibited. Final settlement deductions must be for specifically authorized purposes and cannot be used as a blanket penalty mechanism.
Standard counter-clause: "Deductions from final settlement shall be limited to: verified outstanding loans advanced by the Company, unreturned Company assets at current market value, and training cost recovery under Section [X]. Any disputed deduction shall be credited to the Employee pending resolution of the dispute."

## Clause: No Severance for Redundancy
Act: Industrial Disputes Act 1947, Sections 25C–25F; Companies Act 2013 (for large layoffs)
Contract type: employment
Requirement: Workers retrenched due to redundancy (not misconduct) are entitled to retrenchment compensation (15 days' pay per year of service), proper notice, and government permission (for establishments with 100+ workers). A clause excluding all severance for redundancy is void.
Standard counter-clause: "In the event of termination due to redundancy or organizational restructuring, the Employee shall receive retrenchment compensation as per the Industrial Disputes Act 1947, in addition to the notice period or wages in lieu, and a severance payment of [additional amount] as an ex-gratia."

## Clause: No Mention of Earned Leave Encashment on Death
Act: Payment of Wages Act 1936; Industrial Disputes Act 1947; General labour law principles
Contract type: employment
Requirement: On the death of an employee, all accumulated earned leave and statutory dues must be paid to the nominee or legal heir. The contract should reference the nominee declaration process.
Standard counter-clause: "In the event of the Employee's death during service, all accrued wages, earned leave encashment, gratuity, EPF balance, and other statutory dues shall be paid to the nominee declared by the Employee, or to the legal heirs if no nomination is on record."

## Clause: Work Product Ownership in Collaborative Projects
Act: Copyright Act 1957, Section 13, 17; Patents Act 1970
Contract type: employment
Requirement: When work is created by multiple employees or jointly with a client, ownership of the resulting IP must be expressly allocated. Silence creates ambiguity and potential disputes. Co-authored works have specific rules under the Copyright Act.
Standard counter-clause: "In cases where deliverables are created jointly by the Employee and any client or third party, IP ownership shall be as expressly agreed in the relevant project agreement or statement of work. In the absence of such agreement, IP created solely by the Employee shall vest in the Employer, and jointly created IP shall be owned 50:50 pending a written agreement."
