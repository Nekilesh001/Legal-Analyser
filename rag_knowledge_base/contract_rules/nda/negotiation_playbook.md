# NDA — Negotiation Playbook

## Clause: Overly Broad Definition of Confidential Information (Everything Is Confidential)
Act: Indian Contract Act 1872, Section 29 (certainty of contract)
Contract type: nda
Requirement: An NDA that declares "all information exchanged between parties is confidential" creates an unworkable obligation. Courts in India require that contractual terms be certain. A well-scoped definition protects what genuinely needs protection while being practically enforceable.
Standard counter-clause: "Confidential Information shall be limited to information that is: (a) expressly marked 'Confidential' or 'Proprietary' at the time of disclosure; (b) orally disclosed and confirmed in writing within five business days as confidential; or (c) of a nature that a reasonable business professional would understand to be confidential given the context of disclosure."

## Clause: Perpetual Confidentiality Obligation for All Information
Act: Indian Contract Act 1872; restraint of trade jurisprudence
Contract type: nda
Requirement: A perpetual confidentiality obligation for ordinary business information (not trade secrets) is difficult to enforce and creates indefinite legal exposure. Courts in India have found disproportionately long obligations unreasonable.
Standard counter-clause: "Confidentiality obligations shall continue for a period of three years from the date of disclosure of the relevant information, except for Trade Secrets (as specifically identified by the Disclosing Party at time of disclosure) which shall be protected for the duration of their status as trade secrets."

## Clause: Unilateral Right to Modify NDA Terms
Act: Indian Contract Act 1872, Section 10 (free consent)
Contract type: nda
Requirement: A clause allowing one party to amend the NDA without the other party's consent undermines the bilateral nature of the agreement and may make the NDA illusory. Both parties must consent to material changes.
Standard counter-clause: "This agreement may only be amended by a written instrument signed by duly authorized representatives of both parties. No oral modification, course of dealing, or usage of trade shall modify this agreement."

## Clause: NDA Covers Third-Party Information Without Permission
Act: Indian Contract Act 1872; Copyright Act 1957
Contract type: nda
Requirement: Sharing a third party's confidential information under an NDA without the third party's consent may breach that third party's rights. The NDA should require that each party only disclose its own confidential information (or information it is legally authorized to disclose).
Standard counter-clause: "Each party represents and warrants that it is duly authorized to disclose any Confidential Information it provides under this agreement, that such disclosure does not violate any obligation to any third party, and that the receiving party's use of such information for the Permitted Purpose does not infringe any third-party rights."

## Clause: No Carve-Out for Legal Advice and Privilege
Act: Evidence Act 1872, Sections 126–129 (attorney-client privilege)
Contract type: nda
Requirement: Legal advice shared between a party and its lawyers is privileged and should not be swept into confidentiality disclosures. The NDA should recognize legal privilege and not require disclosure of legally privileged communications.
Standard counter-clause: "Nothing in this agreement shall require either party to disclose information that is subject to legal professional privilege or attorney-client privilege. Both parties acknowledge that they may seek and act on legal advice concerning this agreement without being required to share such advice."

## Clause: Broad Employee Non-Solicitation Without Time Limit
Act: Indian Contract Act 1872, Section 27
Contract type: nda
Requirement: Non-solicitation of employees clauses without a time limit are analogous to perpetual non-compete clauses and are challengeable under Section 27. A reasonable time limit (12–24 months) is both commercially appropriate and legally defensible.
Standard counter-clause: "The non-solicitation of employees restriction shall apply for a period of twelve months from the date of last disclosure of Confidential Information or from the termination of this agreement, whichever is later. Hiring through open public advertisements does not constitute solicitation under this clause."

## Clause: Assignability of NDA Without Consent
Act: Indian Contract Act 1872, Section 37
Contract type: nda
Requirement: Allowing assignment of NDA rights to a third party without consent effectively means the receiving party's obligations can be transferred to an unknown entity that may be a competitor or have no legitimate interest in the information.
Standard counter-clause: "Neither party may assign this agreement or any rights or obligations herein without the prior written consent of the other party. Any purported assignment without such consent shall be null and void. Notwithstanding the foregoing, either party may assign this agreement to a wholly-owned subsidiary without consent, provided the assignee assumes all obligations herein."

## Clause: No Acknowledgment of Adequacy of Damages
Act: Specific Relief Act 1963; Civil Procedure Code, Order 39
Contract type: nda
Requirement: Without an acknowledgment that breach would cause irreparable harm, a party seeking an injunction must prove this in court every time, which is slow and uncertain. An advance acknowledgment is standard practice and strengthens the disclosing party's position.
Standard counter-clause: "Each party expressly acknowledges and agrees that: (a) breach of this agreement would cause the Disclosing Party irreparable harm; (b) monetary damages alone would be an inadequate remedy; and (c) the Disclosing Party shall be entitled to seek injunctive relief without proof of actual damages and without the requirement to post a bond."

## Clause: NDA Signed by Unauthorized Signatory
Act: Companies Act 2013, Section 179; Indian Contract Act 1872, Section 230
Contract type: nda
Requirement: An NDA is only binding if signed by a person duly authorized to bind the company — typically a director (for companies) or a partner (for LLPs). An NDA signed by a regular employee without board authority may not be legally binding on the company.
Standard counter-clause: "This agreement is entered into by duly authorized representatives of each party. Each signatory represents and warrants that they have full power and authority to bind their respective organization to the obligations herein. Proof of authorization (board resolution or equivalent) shall be provided on request."

## Clause: No Dispute Resolution Mechanism Specified
Act: Arbitration and Conciliation Act 1996; Civil Procedure Code
Contract type: nda
Requirement: Without a specified dispute resolution mechanism, parties default to regular civil courts, which are slow and expensive for enforcing NDA obligations. A fast-track arbitration or urgent mediation clause is more practical for NDA disputes.
Standard counter-clause: "Any dispute arising from this agreement shall be subject to expedited arbitration under the rules of [DIAC/MCIA/ICC India] by a sole arbitrator in [city]. The arbitrator shall have the power to grant interim relief including injunctions on an urgent basis within 48 hours of request."

## Clause: Information Shared Verbally Not Protected
Act: Indian Contract Act 1872; trade secret principles
Contract type: nda
Requirement: An NDA that only protects written information leaves a significant gap — most sensitive information is shared in meetings and phone calls. Verbal disclosures should be covered, typically with a short window (5–7 days) to confirm in writing.
Standard counter-clause: "Confidential Information includes oral disclosures, provided the Disclosing Party identifies such information as confidential at the time of disclosure and provides a written summary to the Receiving Party within five business days. Parties may mutually agree in writing to waive this written confirmation requirement for specific ongoing interactions."

## Clause: Return of Information Limited to Physical Documents Only
Act: IT Act 2000; data protection best practices
Contract type: nda
Requirement: A return-of-information clause that only requires return of physical documents leaves electronic copies — emails, shared drives, downloaded files — unaddressed. The clause must explicitly cover electronic copies and certified deletion.
Standard counter-clause: "Upon termination or on request, the Receiving Party shall: (a) return or destroy all physical copies of Confidential Information; (b) permanently delete all electronic copies from all systems including cloud storage, backup systems, and employee personal devices used for work; (c) provide a written certification of such deletion within ten business days signed by an authorized officer."

## Clause: NDA Governs IP Ownership in Collaborative Evaluation
Act: Copyright Act 1957; Patents Act 1970; Indian Contract Act 1872
Contract type: nda
Requirement: During a joint evaluation or proof-of-concept phase where both parties create something collaboratively, the NDA should specify that jointly created work product does not transfer IP ownership to either party by default.
Standard counter-clause: "Any work product, analysis, or other materials created jointly by both parties during the evaluation period shall not be owned by either party and may not be used commercially by either party without a separate written IP agreement. Each party's pre-existing IP used in the evaluation remains solely the property of the originating party."

## Clause: No Notification Obligation for Data Breach
Act: IT Act 2000, Section 43A; IT (Reasonable Security Practices) Rules 2011
Contract type: nda
Requirement: If the receiving party suffers a security breach that may have exposed the disclosing party's confidential information, the NDA should require prompt notification. The IT Rules require notification for sensitive personal data breaches.
Standard counter-clause: "In the event of any actual or reasonably suspected unauthorized access, disclosure, or loss of Confidential Information, the Receiving Party shall notify the Disclosing Party within 72 hours of becoming aware, describing the nature of the incident, the information affected, and the remediation steps taken or planned."

## Clause: Consideration for NDA Not Specified (Risk of Unenforceability)
Act: Indian Contract Act 1872, Section 25 (agreement without consideration is void)
Contract type: nda
Requirement: A standalone NDA must have consideration to be enforceable. Common considerations include: the mutual exchange of confidential information, a payment, or an undertaking to enter into negotiations. NDAs entered into without any consideration may be challenged as void.
Standard counter-clause: "In consideration of the mutual exchange of Confidential Information and the mutual obligations herein, the sufficiency of which both parties acknowledge, each party agrees to be bound by the terms of this agreement. [Alternative: In consideration of the payment of INR [amount] by [party] to [other party], receipt of which is acknowledged...]"
